LifeAlly

Product strategy and concept design

The Client

LifeAlly - the new fun and social health resource, helping people optimise healthy daily lifestyle choices through the motivation of gamification in a highly social context.

Timeline

2 weeks

The Team

Arielle Cook, Lorraine Chung

Skills, Techniques and Tools used

  • Competitive & comparitive analysis
  • Business analysis
  • User research
  • Personas
  • Affinity mapping
  • User flows
  • Sitemaps
  • Concept development
  • Usability testing
  • Stakeholder management
  • Documentation
  • Branding concepts

In Summary – The Brief

My team was to enhance the user experience to create user desire to join LifeAlly and facilitate easy entry into the program.

In Summary – The Solution

We improved the conceptual design of the visitor landing page and the on-boarding process. We re-organised the user flow to become a more engaging and streamlined experience.

Researching the Business and Competitive Landscape

We reviewed the current LifeAlly site and identified four major areas with opportunities for improvement:

  • Nature of the content/information provided
  • Overall information architecture
  • On-boarding process
  • Visual design

We then research competitors to find what on-boarding processes, gamification, and community-based features are typically used to engage consumers.

We discovered that LifeAlly holds a niche as it doesn't align itself with weight loss programs, and instead focuses on a balanced lifestyle (mentally and physically). At the core of this is its LifeIQ quiz.

Interviewing Users

We conducted 18 interviews to get a clearer sense of what motivates people, what prevents them from a healthier lifestyle, and of their experience with existing health and wellness products.

Card Sorting and Information Architecture

I conducted a card sorting exercise with 10 potential users to find out how to best organise the content on the website. The outcome was a set of categories and subcategories. The key insights I found from this was that people wanted to have a category with suggested toys, and to have the ability to search by brand and age.

Analysis and Ideation

We found recurring themes in people's feelings and behaviours towards health and wellness programs.

We analysed how LifeAlly's features and functions addressed these feelings and behaviours, and identified ways to close the gapes. We came up with concepts and features that would increase engagement with the users and maximise the product value.

From Sketches to Prototype

Using the features prioritised, we sketched interfaces to support first-time users from the initial landing page, through the on-boarding process to their personal dashboard.

After testing and iterating our paper prototypes, I implemented the sketches into a low-fi interactive prototype using Illustrator and Invision.

The Outcome

We delivered a detailed research report containing key insights and suggestions that would add considerable value to the product.

LifeAlly has confirmed that they will use this report to guide the company's future design direction. Our client was so pleased with my team's results they are considering engaging us to refine the concept further.
