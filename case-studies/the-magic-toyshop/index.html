The Magic Toyshop

An e-commerce site

The Client

This concept project was completed during my User Experience Design Immersive course at General Assembly (December 2014)

Timeline

2 weeks

The Team

Arielle Cook

Skills, Techniques and Tools used

  • Card sorting (ConceptCodify - card sorting software)
  • Information architecture
  • Sitemap
  • User flows
  • Sketching
  • Wireframes
  • Lo-fi prototyping (Photoshop, InDesign, Illustrator)
  • Usability testing
  • Invision

In Summary – The Brief

The Magic Toy Shop is a retailer of toys, games and magic tricks, specialising in sourcing the most cherished items from everyone's childhood. It is based in Balmain, Sydney.

The brief was to design a traditional, charming e-commerce site that replicates the service and feel of the shop.

In Summary – The Solution

After conducting a competitive analysis, contextual inquiry in other toy shops, and user research, it became clear that the most important characteristics of the solution were delivering a Personal Experience and easy navigation.

I have conducted an online competitive analysis between small boutique and large chain stores.

Understanding the Competitive Landscape

I have conducted an online competitive analysis between small boutique and large chain stores. The objective was to gain an understanding of how the brand positioning, information architecture, layout designs and search functions varied from site to site.

Toy stores have flooded the online market. However, there is room for The Magic Toy Store to fill a niche in the vintage, more personalised toy market, as seen in the figure opposite.

Card Sorting and Information Architecture

I conducted a card sorting exercise with 10 potential users to find out how to best organise the content on the website. The outcome was a set of categories and subcategories. The key insights I found from this was that people wanted to have a category with suggested toys, and to have the ability to search by brand and age.

Sitemaps and User Flows

After completing the card sorting exercise with potential users, I collated the results and formed the arrangement of the site (as reflected in the sitemap).

I used the sitemap as a way to test the navigational experience of a user through the site.

Sketching, Wireframing, Low-Fi Prototyping and Testing

Armed with the sitemap and user flows, I started sketching some initial concepts. These were iteratively refined and turned into a low-fi interactive prototype using Illustrator and Invision.

This prototype was then tested with 8 people who would match the target audience of the site.

The Outcome

I analysed the results from the usability testing in light of Nielsen's 5 qualities of usability. The results show the following:

  • EFFICIENCY - The checkout process is simple and seamless
  • SATISFACTION - The post-purchase message is positive and encourages users to continue browsing the site

However the results also show areas that need improvement:

  • LEARNABILITY - Make the menu items on the navigation bar more descriptive
  • MEMORABILITY - Add an option to review past purchases in the Account information section of the site
  • ERROR MANAGEMENT - Positive feedback on the ability to request a toy if a search shows no results
