In Summary – The Brief

Cabcharge approached us with two primary objectives:

  1. To create a travel management system in response to their clients needs for better administration and control over Cabcharge products. Cabcharge also wanted this system to enhance their products functionality and usability beyond their competitors.
  2. Introduce and implement an agile approach to product design, delivery and collaboration within the company.

In Summary – The Solution

  • Create a complementary online application to support the management of Cabcharge corporate travel. The key focus was to create better visibility of employee travel and spend to help solve and combat the increasing occurrence of travel fraud and misuse.
  • Create a high performing agile delivery team to support the Corporate Accounts business.

I started off by talking to key stakeholders within the business in order to better understand the their challenges and vision. Key learnings were:

  • Cabcharge has over 40,000 clients using their tickets and corporate travel cards.
  • The business unit relies on client retention and the long term customer relationships which, of late, had been under considerable pressure due to competitor offerings and the stigma of fraud and misuse associated with their Cabcharge products.

Together we identified and aligned on expectations and were able to construct a shared overall vision of what the travel management system would encompass.

We then took this vision into the research phase to confirm or disapprove our hypothesis of why a travel management system was needed.

I reviewed all of Cabcharges offerings to understand their business model and overall user experience.

Cabcharge have 5 main product offerings:

Fast card

A reusable card that is linked to an individual user

E-ticket

A single use paper-based electronic ticket

FLEXe-ticket

A single use paper based electronic ticket with dollar, time and date restrictions

Giftcards

Cabcharge Taxi Management System

  • The system provides online downloadable statements
  • It can keep track of all issued Cabcharge Cards or eTICKETs, the time, date, trip costs and description.

Contextual inquiries

Before jumping into a solution driven driven by the business and competitor research findings, we set out to perform a set of contextual inquiries and interviews with Cabcharge's top 10 clients (Which became our test pilot users that we would continuously engage with to get feedback and refine our designs). We engaged them with a list of questions and system activities in order to understand their pain points and better define their needs.

Key Findings

  • As Cabcharge’s CTMS system was very out of date in terms of functionality, look and feel, users found it difficult to use and not as user friendly or intuitive as its competitors tools.
  • We discovered that the administration burden associated with cabcharge products was a more frequent recurring pain point than that of fraud and misuse.

Personas

Through careful analysis of our research, we identified sufficient behavioural variables to segment our user audience and were able to create a set of 6 personas:

  • Ben – The custodian, department manager and end user of Cabcharge products.
  • Elise – The overseer of all travel use within her company.
  • Amber – The cost centre manager and custodian of all cab charge products.
  • Darren – The frequent tech-savvy traveller.
  • Kate – The frequent not-so-tech savvy traveller.
  • Michael – The not-so-tech savvy infrequent traveller.

Customer journey maps

Looking at the research from our contextual inquiries I created an overall customer journey map to visualise and communicate the users end‐to‐end experience across various touch‐points of Cabcharge products.

These maps indicated to us that we were to focus our attention on the three most common pain points discovered:

  • Administration
  • Fraud
  • Misuse

Feature Prioritisation

Armed with all our research I entered all the features into a spreadsheet and prioritised them against our 3 focus areas, personas needs, tech feasibility, and business objectives.

Information Architecture

Having a list of prioritised features, I conducted a card sorting activity with a combination of user types to find out how to best organise the content on the website.

The outcome was a set of categories and subcategories.

Key Insights

  • Different types of users interpreted the content in different ways, indicating that we needed to segment the content into 3 types of access levels to not overwhelm them with irrelevant content.

After collating our results from the card sorting exercise I was able to construct a site map that was adaptable to our 3 access types while also being logical, navigable, and intuitive to our users.

As we were working in an agile 2 week sprint format, we started by off developing the highest prioritised features off the backlog to create an MVP for launch.

I would use the following design development cycle to ensure selected features were successfully deployed at the end of the sprint. This process also gave us the confidence that when launched, the travel management system would work as intended, and be simple, engaging and fun to use.

The development of an online travel management system called Cabcharge plus. A system that helps managers by removing a considerable amount of administration, travel fraud and misuse that was once associated with cabcharge products

Visual Design

In addition to delivering the UX experience for a responsive site, I was engaged to create new logo, look and feel for Cabcharge plus. After conducting a brand analysis I created a look that was modern, fresh, pain-free, softer, simple and professional.

Achievements

  • The stakeholders within Cabcharge loved the new look so much that they decided to implement across the new development of the main website, all future print/media material and taxi terminals.
  • The creation of a cloud based application, Cabcharge Plus, which is now available to over 40,000 clients Australia wide.
