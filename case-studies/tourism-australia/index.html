Tourism Australia

Giga Selfie event and microsite creation

The Client

Tourism Australia, the Australian Goverment statuatory authority, tasked with the promotion of Australia as an international tourism destination.

The Team

Being the sole UX designer on this project, I worked alongside the Client, Project Manager, Developers, Copywriters and Visual Designers to bring this project to life.

Skills, Techniques and Tools used

  • User Journeys
  • Information Architecture
  • Sketching
  • Wireframes (Axure)
  • Stakeholder Management

In Summary – The Brief

Tourism Australia's Giga Selfie is a world-leading photography experience. The Giga Selfie is a video showcasing a close-up image of a person zooming out to a panoramic landscape view.

Tourism Australia were launching the experience at Dawes Point in Sydney Harbour for the 2016 Chinese New Year celebrations

I was briefed to create the best possible uer journeys for the event itself, a microsite, iPad web app, and follow-up eDM.

In Summary – The Solution

After conducting a review of the suggested process, it became clear that I would refine and streamline the user experience from the previously designed experience.

I started off by conducting research amongst the types of people that would be attending the event in order to better my understanding of the target audience, and inform my design decisions throughout the project.

As this event would include 50% Chinese and 50% Australian users, this heavily influenced the technical requirements (e.g. which apps would be used for video download) and the overall user journey to accommodate Chinese-speaking users.

I constructed a user journey map to show a step-by-step process of the new, streamlined epxerience to internal and external stakeholders.

Developers were then introduced to the project, and I worked with them to thoroughly scrutinise the process. After a series of iterations, I fine-tuned the experience to incorporate the additional technical requirements that came up during this phase.

Finalising the user journey helped me identify the key screens that needed to be designed for the microsite and web app.
