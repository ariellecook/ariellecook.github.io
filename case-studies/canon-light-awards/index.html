Canon Light Awards

A responsive microsite and a strategically driven marketing landing page

The Client

Canon Australia, the leading producer of Digital Cameras, SLR Cameras, Professional Printers, Scanners & Fax Machines for both business & home users.

Timeline

1 week

The Team

Being the sole UX designer on this project, I worked alongside the Client, Project Manager, Developers, Copywriters and Visual Designers to bring this project to life.

Skills, Techniques and Tools used

  • Information Architecture
  • Sketching
  • Wireframes (Axure)
  • User Research
  • Concept Development
  • Stakeholder Management

In Summary – The Brief

Canon Light Awards is an ongoing photographic competition run by Canon. The business would like to see more entries, subscribers and value extracted out of the program. The objective was to increase signups to the Canon Light Awards database through an eDM, My Canon and social mediums.

In Summary – The Solution

To create an engaging and compelling landing page with the sole purpose to encourage users to join the Canon Light Awards community by signing up.

The Target Audience

I began by researching the target audience to establish what challenges we would face when encouraging people to sign up.

I found that the audience ranged from amateur to experienced photographers alike. This highlighted some known barriers for entry, such as "I'm not good enough", "You need high-end equipment to stand a chance" and "This competition is for professionals only".

Prototyping

I addressed the above issues by using encouraging copy highlighted on the homepage.

I started sketching out ideas and then translating them into an interactive prototype in Axure – this was to explicitly show specific interactions within the wireframes in order to aid the development team during the production phase.
