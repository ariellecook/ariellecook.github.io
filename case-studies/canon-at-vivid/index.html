Canon at Vivid

A responsive event marketing microsite

We've never seen such clear and easy to follow wireframes before, we were really impressed with the work. They were even easy enough for out non-digital-savvy stakeholders to understand.

The Client

Canon Australia, the leading producer of Digital Cameras, SLR Cameras, Professional Printers, Scanners & Fax Machines for both business & home users.

The Team

Being the sole UX designer on this project, I worked alongside the Client, Strategist, Project Manager, Developers, Copywriters and Visual Designers to bring this project to life.

Skills, Techniques and Tools used

  • Information Architecture
  • Sitemap
  • User Flows
  • Sketching
  • Wireframes (Omnigraffle)
  • Competitive & Comparitive Analysis
  • User Research
  • Concept Development
  • Stakeholder Management

In Summary – The Brief

Vivid Sydney is an 18 day festival of light, music and ideas, and for the second year running Canon is a proud sponsor. The brief was to design a platform to display educational, informational, and social content designed to engage consumers in Canon, pre-, during, and post-Vivid.

Competitive and Comparative Analysis

I researched competitor sites to define the market scope, familiarise myself with competitor sites and find what on-boarding process and community-based features are typically used to engage consumers.

This project had such a broad target audience that it needed to work for many different personas. I took that knowledge and refined my research findings to deliver an encouraging, inspiring and simple responsive website.

Site Maps

Being supplied with the site content, I began structuring a simple site map.

User Journeys

I used the sitemap to conceptualise and then define all possible user journeys to visualise all the relevant functionality of the site.

Wireframes

Having identified 6 key screens through the sitemap, I created wireframs. I worked on reformatting the content of each screen from the supplied content, and simplified the information and interactions between each of the pages.

Developers were then introduced to the project, and I worked alongside them to thoroughly scrutinise the website and prepare it for development. I went through a series of iterations on the wireframes, taking into account new considerations that arose as we fine-tuned the design.

The wireframes were then brought to life with inspiring images and a striking interface design.

The Outcome

Post event, the site continues to serve as a destination for all Canon at Vivid related content. Furthermore, this continues to serve as a UX framework for new and additional Canon projects.
