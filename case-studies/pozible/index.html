Pozible

A mobile app

The Client

This concept project was completed during my User Experience Design Immersive course at General Assembly (December 2014)

Timeline

1 week

The Team

Arielle Cok, Tertia Pentz, John Havel

Skills, Techniques and Tools used

  • Card sorting (ConceptCodify - card sorting software)
  • Information architecture
  • Sitemap
  • User flows
  • Sketching
  • Wireframes
  • Lo-fi prototyping (Photoshop, InDesign, Illustrator)
  • Usability testing
  • Invision

In Summary – The Brief

1. Crowdfunding is the practice of funding a project or venture by raising monetary conributions from a large number of people, typically via the internet.

2. Pozible is an Australian crowdfunding platform and community for creative projects and ideas. Developed for artists, musicians, filmmakers, journalists, designers, etc.

My team's challenge was to determine how Pozible could expand their platform to include projects that involve recurring payments.

In Summary – The Solution

My team's research and analysis led to our conclusion that Pozible should support not only one-off creative projects, but also charitable initiatives with a recurrent application – therefore increasing the scope of Pozible projects and its platform by accepting recurring pledges.

Understanding the Competitive Landscape

My team reviewed Pozible's website to understand its functionality, business model and user experience. We then conducted a competitive analysis to contrast the key features, projects, and digital platforms of Pozible's competitors. We discovered:

  • Pozible had created a niche platform in its focus on only creative projects
  • Some of Pozible's competitors supported recurring funding
  • Pozible provides a responsive website for its projects, while its competitors also include an app

Surveys and Interviews

We conducted interviews (15+ people) and distributed an online survey (30+ responses) to learn about people's behavious and views, and to test our hypothesis.

We learned how important community is as people give to those they know, and like to give to charities on a continual basis.

This supported our hypothesis that charitable projects, which are ripe for recurring contributions, could be a useful extension to Pozible's platform.

Creating Personas

My team created two personas to validate potential features and drive our design.

  • Steve, the creator
  • Lisa, the pledger

Using Steve and Lisa, we created a persona empathy map to help understand their pain points.

Feature Prioritisation

Using the pain points from our personas we came up with over 20 ideas to resolve these problems, prioritising the best ideas on their levels of technical difficulty.

Based on this analysis, we focused on an app optimised for pledgers.

Why an App?

  • An app is easier to access instantly and share information with others.
  • Gives people instant, on-the-go notifications.

User Flows and Sitemap

We created a sitemap to test a user's navigational experience of the app.

From Sketches to Prototype

We sketched interfaces to suppoert first-time users searching for and pledging towards a project. We tested these designs on paper then created wireframes and built an interactive prototype (using Axure) for more rigorous usability testing.

Testing and Design

Using the interactive prototype, through design iterations I took the team's research even further.

After conducting a brand analysis, I developed a visual concept of the app (leveraging the design of the current website). Changes were made to the wireframes to reflect my findings.

The Outcome

The greatest validation of my team's solution was that shortly after completing this project, Pozible redesigned their business model to include recurring community- and charitable-based projects. This change was coherent with our research insights.
