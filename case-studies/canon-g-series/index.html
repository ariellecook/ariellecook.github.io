Canon G-Series

A gamified product driven microsite

The Client

Canon Australia, the leading producer of Digital Cameras, SLR Cameras, Professional Printers, Scanners & Fax Machines for both business & home users.

Timeline

1 week

The Team

Being the sole UX designer on this project, I worked alongside the Client, Project Manager, Developers, and Visual Designers to bring this project to life.

Skills, Techniques and Tools used

  • Information Architecture
  • User Flows
  • Sketching
  • Wireframes (Axure)
  • Competitive & Comparitive Analysis
  • User Research
  • Stakeholder Management

In Summary – The Brief

Owning and carrying a compact camera is considered onerous, and the category is losing relevance due to smartphones making it increasingly difficult for manufacturers like Canon to compete. The G-Series is a new range of compact cameras that offer a technology solution that outperforms smartphone technology and offers users a chance to take great photographs at near-DSLR standard.

Canon wanted us to build a microsite to market the G-Series range to consumers without focusing solely on tech specs and performance stats.

Business Obejctives

  • Establish the Canon G-Series as the #1 high-performance DSC range in market.
  • Achieve #1 value share in high-performance DSC in 2H 2015.
  • Grow Canon DSC brand performance in key measure – innovation.

In Summary – The Solution

Research showed that we would be targeting a young, socially connected audience who were looking for products that matched their style. It was found that the design and aesthetic of their camera was as important, if not more, than the technology.

We needed a creative solution that was fun, a bit off the wall and not take itself too seriously. Our solution: "Which G-Series are you?", A microsite that engages consumers to find their perfect G-Series camera through a series of questions and vibrant imagery.

This enabled us to bring out series of camera personalities and to communicate the product benefits of each individual G-Series compact without reverting to a list of specifications.
