HCF

A responsive package comparison site

The Client

HCF, one of Australia's largest combined registered private health fund and life insurance organisations.

Timeline

UX: 1 week
Design: 2 weeks

The Team

Working as a UX and Graphic Designer on this project, I worked alongside the Client, Project Manager, and Developers to bring this project to life.

Skills, Techniques and Tools used

  • Information Architecture
  • User Flows
  • Sketching
  • Wireframes (Illustrator)
  • Graphic Design (Photoshop & Illustrator)
  • Prototyping (Invision)
  • Stakeholder Management

In Summary – The Brief

Each year, health funds advise their customers of the new increased health rates for the next 12 months due to government regulations and health cost inflation.

We were briefed to design a landing page that gave customers health package alternatives. This was to demonstrate that HCF were not trying to rip-off customers with their rate increases.

In Summary – The Solution

A responsive web page for both mobile and desktop that displayed a personalised comparison tool (landing page) showing customers their current cover, with two side-by-side comparisons (one more expensive, one cheaper) that allowed customers to compare both the price and benefits.

After many rounds of usability testing and UX iteration, I brought the wireframes to life in design with HCF's new brand look and feel. Throughout the graphic design phase, I kept in mind that this was HCF's first major customer mailing that showcased their new brand look and feel, so it was important that any communications accurately adhered to the new brand and language guidelines.
